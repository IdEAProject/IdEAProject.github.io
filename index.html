IdEA

Interdisciplinary Exchange of the Americas

What is IdEA?

We are a group of students affiliated with Stanford’s Haas Center for Public Service seeking to use our skills and passion for technology to better the lives of those around us. Thanks to recent political advances, it’s now more viable than ever for American organizations to run technical and educational workshops in Cuba with this goal in mind.

Given this opportunity, we are striving to create a week-long workshop focusing on need-finding within Cuban institutions, culminating in a week-long interdisciplinary exchange of ideas in summer 2015. In particular, we believe that we can deliver the highest impact in developing technologies for the Cuban hospital and/or the public education systems.

Why Cuba?

 

Cuba faces a unique set of technical challenges, with internet penetration rate estimated at below 5%. The country has well-educated population, with one of the worlds highest literacy rates, but with little access to state-of-the-art information technology. Locals describe a widespread "hunger" for exposure to modern technologies, especially among college students.

We believe there exists a unique opportunity to work within the existing economic and political infrastructure to help advance education in tech and even assist in building out technological solutions to everyday issues.
