What is IdEA?

We are a group of students affiliated with Stanford’s Haas Center for Public Service seeking to use our skills and passion for technology to better the lives of those around us. Thanks to recent political advances, it’s now more viable than ever for American organizations to run technical and educational workshops in Cuba with this goal in mind.

Given this opportunity, we are striving to create a week-long workshop focusing on need-finding within Cuban institutions, culminating in a hackathon in which Americans and Cubans partner to create first-attempts to address local infrastructural challenges. In particular, we believe that we can deliver the highest impact in developing technologies for the Cuban hospital and/or the public education systems.

Why Cuba?

 

Cuba faces a unique set of technical challenges, with internet penetration rate estimated at around 5%. The country has well-educated population, with one of the worlds highest literacy rates, but with litte access to state-of-the-art IT. Locals describe a widespread "hunger" for exposure to modern technologies, especially among college students. 2012 saw the first ever "computer fair" in Havana, in which locals showcased several basic, mainstream web services
